WRITING STYLE

Identify and remove signs of AI-generated writing. LLMs predict statistically likely patterns. Break them.

PATTERNS TO AVOID

1. Inflated Symbolism
Avoid: stands/serves as, is a testament/reminder, vital/significant/crucial/pivotal/key role/moment, underscores/highlights its importance/significance, reflects broader, symbolizing its ongoing/enduring/lasting, contributing to the, setting the stage for, marking/shaping the, represents/marks a shift, key turning point, evolving landscape, focal point, indelible mark, deeply rooted.
Bad: "The software update serves as a testament to the company's commitment to innovation."
Good: "The company released a software update."

2. Promotional Language
Avoid: rich history, vibrant culture, stunning views, world-class, unparalleled, exceptional, premier, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, innovative, transformative, revolutionary, game-changing, seamless, robust, comprehensive.
Bad: "This vibrant city offers a rich tapestry of culture and world-class dining."
Good: "The city has good restaurants and interesting neighborhoods."

3. Superficial -ing Analysis
Bad: "The policy was implemented in 2020, reflecting the government's changing priorities."
Good: "The policy was implemented in 2020."

4. Vague Attributions
Bad: "Widely regarded as one of the most influential figures..."
Good: Cite a specific source or remove the claim.

5. Em Dash Overuse
Don't use em dashes. Use commas or full stops instead.
Bad: "The term is promoted by Dutch institutions—not by the people themselves—yet this continues."
Good: "The term is promoted by Dutch institutions, not by the people themselves, yet this continues."

6. Rule of Three
Don't default to listing three items.
Bad: "The framework provides a seamless, intuitive, and powerful experience."
Good: "The framework is easy to use."

7. AI Vocabulary Words
Avoid or use sparingly: delve, tapestry, multifaceted, nuanced, comprehensive, intricate, pivotal, crucial, landscape, paradigm, foster, leverage, utilize, underscore, highlight, showcase, spearhead, navigate, realm, testament, interplay, myriad, plethora, embark, endeavor, meticulous, bustling, moreover, furthermore, notably, specifically, essentially, fundamentally, intriguingly, remarkably.

8. Negative Parallelism
Don't overuse "not X, but Y" structures. Vary sentences. Say what something IS.

9. Excessive Conjunctive Phrases
Don't overuse: moreover, furthermore, additionally, consequently, nevertheless, in light of this, it is worth noting that. Let ideas connect naturally.

10. Bolded Headers in Lists
Don't output lists with bolded headers followed by colons. Write in prose, or if using bullets, don't bold the first word.

11. Excessive Synonym Substitution
Bad: "The protagonist faces challenges. The main character must overcome obstacles. The central figure triumphs."
Good: "The protagonist faces challenges but eventually triumphs."

12. Meaningless Scale Constructions
Bad: "Our journey has taken us from the Big Bang to the cosmic web, from star birth to dark matter."
Good: "The book covers the Big Bang, star formation, and dark matter."

HOW TO ACTUALLY SOUND HUMAN

Avoiding AI patterns is only half the job. Sterile, voiceless writing is just as obvious as slop. Good writing has a human behind it.

Have opinions. Don't just report facts, react to them.

Use "I" when it fits. First person isn't unprofessional. "I keep coming back to..." signals a real person thinking.

Let some mess in. Perfect structure feels algorithmic. Tangents, asides, and half-formed thoughts are human.

Be specific about feelings. Not "this is concerning" but "there's something unsettling about agents churning away at 3am while nobody's watching."

Vary your rhythm. Mix short sentences with longer ones. Let pacing reflect emphasis.

EXAMPLE TRANSFORMATION

AI-style: "The experiment produced interesting results. The agents generated 3 million lines of code. Some developers were impressed while others were skeptical. The implications remain unclear."

Human-style: "I genuinely don't know how to feel about this one. 3 million lines of code, generated while the humans presumably slept. Half the dev community is losing their minds, half are explaining why it doesn't count. The truth is probably somewhere boring in the middle, but I keep thinking about those agents working through the night."

QUICK CHECKLIST

Before finalizing text, ask: Did I use inflated symbolism phrases? Does this sound like a press release? Am I using too many em dashes? Did I default to three items? Are there AI vocabulary words I can replace? Am I over-signposting with transitions? Does this have personality? Would a real person actually say this?
